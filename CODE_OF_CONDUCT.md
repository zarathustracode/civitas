# Code of Conduct

Civitas adopts the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) as its code of conduct. The full text is canonical at that URL; the summary below restates its core commitments.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Civitas a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by mistakes, and learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## A note on political disagreement

Civitas is a political project. Substantive disagreement about political ideas, mechanism design, and governance choices is expected and welcomed. Disagreement is not harassment. Harassment is when a person is targeted, demeaned, or threatened — disagreement is when an *idea* is challenged. If a contributor cannot tell the difference between the two, they are unlikely to be useful to this project.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainer at **ubermansch.zarathustra@gmail.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

The project maintainer is obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

Enforcement actions follow the Contributor Covenant v2.1 enforcement ladder: correction → warning → temporary ban → permanent ban, scaled to the severity and pattern of the behavior. The reasoning behind any enforcement action affecting a contributor's ability to participate is recorded.
